Conceptpair
The pair $(x,y)(x,y \in A)$ is defined as a mapping from $set(1,2)$ to $A$ by $( (pairxy)1 ) = x$ and $( (pairxy)2) = y$ The other alternative would be to define the pair by the axiom: $(a,b) = (c,d) \Leftrightarrow a = c \wedge b =d$ We have not made this choice, since then we would have an additional axiom.

Conceptcart-prod
The cartesian product of two sets x and y $(X,Y \subset (OA) )$ is defined as a mapping from $set(1,2)$ to $(OA)$ by $a \in X \times Y$ $\Leftrightarrow \exists x \in X \vee \exists y \in Y$ with $a = (x,y) \forall a \in A$
Definition forallY,X,Aequivalent(cart-prod(X,Y)(A),existsX,Yeq(A,pair(X,Y))) 1
Conceptn-tuple
Like the pair it's possible to define the n-tuple by the projection mappings to the different components. It's useful to have the definition of the n-tuple although x as mapping is already an n-tuple! One consequence of the definition is that two n-tuples could only be equal iff they have the same length and each component is identical.

Conceptn-cart-prod
In analogy to the definition of the cartesian product of two sets we can also define the cartesian product of n sets $X_1 \times X_2 \times ...\times X_n$.
